1. Why zines work

    What is a zine ?

    A zine is a booklet
    made by a transgender
    anarchist who doesn't have
    any other options.
    The Zine is a format
    that's good at containing
    information.
    A zine is a unit, a thing,
    a piece of information.
    A zine is, specifically,
    a chunk of information.
    It is made of + 7 smaller
    chunks of information.

    A page folded thrice makes
    for eight portions; six
    smaller pages and cover :
    this is the classic format.

3.

    The Law of Exactly Sevenish

    Seven has been a number of
    magical and cosmic signifiance
    for all of human history.
    Especially 1956. That's when
    George Miller wrote
    The Magical Number 7, +2.
    The paper exemplains why you can
    remember a telephone number
    but not a credit card number.

    I think Miller's Law is about more than just human short    term memory. What if it applies not to just our brains, but information itself ? What if it's part of a general information theory that we don't have yet ?Regardless, it applies to zines.

4.

    The Theory of Memes

    Before it was rare Soyjaks, the meme was a serious theory by Dawkins '76. He named memetics following genetics, including select    tion. But his evolutionary metaphor didn't even apply to evolution. Don't get me wrong, I like his theory (and evolution.) But Dawkins called a meme a unit of culture. Sounds squishy, doesn't it ? Let's call it instead a chunk of information. Even better, a mirrorable chunk of information : one that can be easily repeated..

For us, by Xerox.

    Shrink To Grow

    Notice that you don't have to read Miller Dawkins and ... uh, Rizzolatti to follow me. That's because their books compress into my mini-page chunks. The behavior of the chunkd is what mattered to Miller, not the number. They compress, nest, and scale.
